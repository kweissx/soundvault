Step 1 — One-Time Setup

This tool talks directly to the official Spotify Web API from your browser. Nothing is sent anywhere else — your data goes straight from Spotify to files on your computer. You just need a free Spotify "app" to get a Client ID:

  1. Open the Spotify Developer Dashboard and log in with your normal Spotify account.
  2. Click Create app. Name it anything (e.g. "Sound Vault Export").
  3. Under Redirect URIs, add exactly:
  4. Under APIs used, tick Web API, save, and copy the Client ID from the app's settings page.

You only do this once — the Client ID is remembered in this browser.

⚠ You opened this file directly from disk (file://). Spotify login can't redirect back here. Serve the folder locally instead, e.g. run python3 -m http.server 8000 in the site folder and open http://127.0.0.1:8000/spotify-export.html.

Step 2 — Connect to Spotify

Paste your Client ID and connect. Spotify will ask you to approve read-only access to your library, playlists, followed artists and top items.

Step 3 — Export Everything

Grabs your followed artists, liked songs, saved albums, every playlist (with all tracks), and your top artists & tracks. Large libraries can take a couple of minutes — Spotify pages data 50 items at a time.

Step 4 — Your Records

Download your archive. The JSON file is the complete record (best for re-importing or long-term keeping); the CSVs open in any spreadsheet app.

Tip: Spotify's API doesn't expose your full multi-year listening history — for that, also request your "Extended streaming history" once from spotify.com/account/privacy (takes up to 30 days, arrives by email). This tool covers everything you've liked, followed and playlisted.
